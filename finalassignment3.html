  • What They Do

    Paleontologists, as most people know, study fossils, their formation, and how long they've been there. What most people do not know is that Paleontology is much more than just fossils. The subdivisions include micropaleontology, paleobotany, palynology, invertebrate paleontology, vertebrate paleontology, paleoanthropology, taphonomy, ichnology, and paleoecology.

  • Recognition

    One famous paleontologist is Earl Douglass. In the 20th century, he excavated fossils in Utah. There, he discovered many fossils of different dinosaurs including: Allosaurus, Apatosaurus, Barosaurus, Camarasaurus, Camptosaurus, Diplodocus, Dryosaurus, Stegosaurus.
